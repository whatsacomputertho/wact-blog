The What's next for FootballSim now that the MVP is complete?  There are various aspects of FootballSim that I think need improvement.  Each of these enhancements vary in terms of their timeline (short-term versus long-term) and their "well-definedness".  Here, I will collect my thoughts following my MVP ​regarding ​next ​steps for FootballSim.

Short-term ​next ​steps

There are a few things I described throughout the initial FootballSim blog series as "what I would have done differently in retrospect".  I think these observations serve as really great low-hanging fruit for me to cover right away. ​ ​I ​went ​ahead ​and ​raised ​issues for each of these findings:

  • Parameterize domain name in fbsim-acme-responder and fbsim-proxy
  • Migrate ​FootballMatchup ​struct into fbsim-core library
  • Combine ​BoxScoreSimulator ​and ​BoxScoreGenerator ​structs
  • Components ​should ​re-size ​for ​mobile ​window

Throughout my initial usage of FootballSim, I noticed that the model would regularly generate some quite unlikely scores, like 19 and 29.  Furthermore, ties seemed to happen much more frequently than they should.  Both of these observations are purely intuitive, but I think upon further investigation they will be measurable and fixable.  I raised a few issues for this:

  • Produce ​score ​distribution ​histogram and ​lookup table
  • Filter ​scores ​through ​frequency ​lookup ​table
  • Estimate overall tie probability, tie probability by skill differential
  • Re-simulate ​game ​in ​the ​event ​of ​a ​tie ​based ​on ​observed ​tie ​probability
  • Validate ​final ​score ​generator ​model

As far as short-term improvements are concerned, this pretty much covers what I was able to determine with the help of my brother.  Going forward, I think it would be good to build up a slightly broader feedback loop.  There are surely things that need improvement that flew over my head, but that others will notice almost immediately.  These are the things I'd like to uncover.  As such, I might consider running some form of alpha testing fairly soon.

Once I gather a decent amount of feedback through alpha testing (and possibly beta testing), I'll plan to release an "official" v1.0.0 release of FootballSim.  In the process, I may need to make some CI/CD enhancements here and there, and establish a branching strategy for my repositories.

Medium-term next steps

I'm starting to plan out a few new capabilities on top of, or in addition to, the existing FootballSim final score generator model.  Thinking in terms of SemVer, I view these capabilities ultimately as minor releases, since they would just extend existing capabilities and not fundamentally re-work the API. ​ ​That said, these would be larger projects that would need to be fleshed out over the course of multiple weeks.

For now, I've just planned a single issue for each project.  I plan to treat these as "epics" which will contain individual stories underneath them for each underlying FootballSim component that needs work related to the larger project.

  1. Implement ​league ​simulation ​capability
  2. Implement ​quarterly ​box score model

The ​first epic, Implement ​league ​simulation ​capability, is inspired by the spreadsheet that my brother and I are using to orchestrate our mini football league.  The spreadsheet lists each team, their skill levels on offense and defense, their logos, their conference, and their division.  It further includes the schedule for the season, the playoff picture, and the playoff bracket, all of which we wrote up entirely manually.  It would be incredibly useful if we could simply declare and/or generate this all automatically.

The second epic, Implement ​quarterly ​box score model, is in the spirit of incrementally drilling down to finer and finer-grained models as I iterate on FootballSim.  What's more, one of the emerging core pillars of FootballSim is narrative context.  We want the ability to analyze the games and develop our own narratives around them, and storylines for our imaginary teams.  Generating a quarter-by-quarter score breakdown will enhance this experience by allowing us to identify fun things like ​late-game comebacks and overtime games.

Long-term next steps

The goal of this entire project has always been to produce a play-by-play model which can generate a full game log, and player statistics.  I've started to write out a high-level epic encapsulating this large-scale goal: Implement play-by-play model.
